Subject: A dudele un an irele

Ven es kumt tzum nutzn ir oder du
Darf men take visn a ver un a vu.

Zikher iz undzer fraye amerike an informeler land un frayndt megn zikh
dudeven biz tzu der zat. Mit kolegn fun yedn elter, gamken.  Ober
staytch libe mentshn, ven men kumt on in a moyshev zekeynim un me hert
mentshn redn tzu fremde ur-bobes un ur-zeydes af aza shteyger --- vos
vilstu -- iz dos geredt bli derekh-eretz un bavayst az der mentsh ken
nit kin mame loshn vi es darf tzu zayn, oder er/zi iz in gantzn an
am-oretz on gefiln. Lomir nor hofn az yene gedudevete ur-bobes un zeydes
zaynen azey eyver-botl az zey hern afile nisht un es art zey afile
nisht.

Als lererin fun yidish heyb ikh on mitn ir -- ikh zog ir tzu mayne
studentn yung un alt -- un ven es vert a bisele  mer frayndlekh, heybn
mir on zikh tzu dudeven. Der lerner fun yidish, muz farsheyn ven es iz
k'day tzu dudeven un ven tzu ireven. un avade un avade ven men kumt tzu
tzu der yidisher literatur.

Sylvia Schildt

From: wolfim@chelsea.ios.com
Subject: Az du kenst an mir nit glanzen...

To Rivke Steinig's mother, a refue shleyme. She is recalling  one of
Elyezer Shteynbarg's parables, Di barsht un der kamash 'The brush and
the gaiter (spat)'.

The opening lines are:

        kish - kash!
        s'tantst a barsht af a kamash!
        shtarker, shtarker giit der tants
        un alts heler vert der glants --
        s'iz tsu kukn a tshikave!

and ends, after the taynes of the haughty gaiter:

        "zayt nisht broyges, reb kamash!"
        entfert im di barsht, "ma rash?
        az du kenst on mit nisht glants,
        meg ikh, bruder, af dir tantsn!"

Meyer Wolf

Subject: Aynlegn di velt

The poet Rokhl Boymvol has a new book _Antkegn dem vos ir zogt_ which is
a celebration of Yiddish idioms, and the August 2 issue of the Forverts
contains a delightful excerpt -- a set of expressions which use the word
"velt".  Highly recommended.

No excerpts in this space.  If you subscribe to the Forverts you've
already seen it.  If you don't you should be a subscriber.  Chana and Yosl
Mlotek are the guiding geniuses of two genuinely valuable features that
appear on alternating weeks: _in der velt fun yidish_ and _perl fun der
yidisher poezie_.  To this long-time reader the Forverts was never as
good as it is right now. (Would that I could say the same for the
ineptly edited English Forward.)
