In letstn paragraf fun mayn notits in Mendele "Two more daytsmerisms"
(5.307.7, April 22, 1996) iz arayngefaln a badoyerlekher drukfeler:  Dos wort
"matrimonial" iz geven gedrukt anshtot _matrilineal_, vi s'hot grdarft zayn.
Kh'bet ale mayne leyeners mir moykhl zayn, un bazundersh khaver Prager.

In the last paragraph of my post "Two more daytshmerisms"  (5..307,7  of April
22, 1996) a regrettable typographical error appeared:  the word "matrimonial"
was printed in place of the correct _matrilineal_.  My apologies to all my
readers and, especially, to khaver Prager.

Zellig Bach

Subject: Request for update on Yiddish in Israel

The  latest issue of Lebens Fragen March-April '96,a Yiddish Journal published
in Israel  had in  it a brief  but disturbing communication on the events
related to Yiddish since the March 3rd declaration by the Knesset supporting
Yiddish .The article reported the government's recent withdrawal of  its
yearly 10,000 dollar contribution to Yiddish programs throughout the world,as
well as  notice of  eviction given by the Sokhnut (Jewish Agency) to a Yiddish
literary publication to move out of its building. Given the recent joy over
the Knesset's resolutions ,these negative actions raise  anxious questions.
Can the Galays or L. Botwinik or someone else close to the scene in Israel
please tell us what is happening? Is there some misunderstanding? Did we
celebrate too soon?

I would appreciate a reply as  soon as possible.

Sarah Traister-Moskovitz
