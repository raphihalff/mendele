Subject: Sender Vaysman, olev hasholem

Azey fil ondenkn in Forvertz vegn Sender Vaysmanen vos iz ersht avek in
der eybikayt mit a vokh tzurik. Ven zey zaynen gevorn khosn-kale, gedenk
ikh, vi mindl gurner flegt im rufn senderl  ikh hob zey etlekhe mol
getrofn in cleveland ohio, vu zey hobn ongefirt di arbeter ring shules
in shtot.

Ikh vil oysdrikn mitgefil tzu mindlen  (vos ikh gedenk zi fun mayne mitl
shul yorn) als porfolk zaynen zey geven eyns un azelkhes - lerer, kultur
tuer - ikh vintsh mindlen zi zol hobn keykhes ibertzutrogn dem umglik un
farlust. ikh bin kmat 9 yor an almoneh, vil ikh oysdruken mayn tifstn
mitgefil.

sylvia schildt
baltimore md

Eyb ir vet zayn in Baltimore/Vashington gegnt oder nit vayt fun di shtet
dem 13tn oktober, 7 azeyger in ovnt, kumt un hert. Hayntike Yidn in
Amerike zayne azelkhe n'gidim, az men fargest vi shver men hot amol
gehorevet, vifl blut men hot fargosn tzu shafn di yunions un azoy
vayter. Di periode iz an umanerkente yerushe far undz alemen.  Un mir
kumen grade di mentshn  di pyonern a greysn kheyv. Dos iz take eykh a
tzaytike teme tzulib di valn un dem nayem yunion vos heyst UNITE. Di
lider vos Shulamis vet zingen zaynen Yidishe klasiker - Triangle Fayer,
Der  Fodem, Grine Kuzine, un azey vayter. Ikh meyn az zi hot eykh mit
zikh gedrukte materialn - verter/muzik. Ir kent zikh eykh shteln in
kontakt mit di Mlotekn (Yidishn Forverts) - zey zayne di emese keners
fun azelkhe inyonim.

Sylvia Schildt

Subject: Yiddish rhyming dictionary

To respond to Alan Zaremba's question about the availability of a
Yiddish rhyming dictionary, in 1931 Nokhem Stutchkov's Yidisher
Gramen-Leksikon (Yiddish Rhyming Lexicon) was published.  I doubt if any
other Yiddish rhyming dictionary came out since then.

Surprisingly, I was able to purchase a copy from the Workmen's Circle,
45 East 33rd Street, New York, NY 10016 (E-mail: wcfriends@aol.com)
about five years ago.  Perhaps they still have some copies left.  It's
quite good, very logically arranged and easy to use.

Al Grand

I'm interested in the lyrics of the yidish song "aroys iz in Vilne".

The first verse from a booklet of a CD is

  aroys iz in Vilne a nayer bafel,
  tsu brengen di yidn fun shtetlekh.
  gebrakht hot men ale,
  fun yunge biz alt,
  afile oykh kranke oyf betlekh.

Since the last verses in the booklet are missing and I don't understand
the singer well enough, I would be very thankful for any help.
